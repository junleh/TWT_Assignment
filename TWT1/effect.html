Effects of Air Pollution

Health Effects

Air pollution contributes to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, such as asthma, bronchitis, and lung cancer. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) and other pollutants can penetrate deep into the lungs, causing inflammation and aggravating existing health conditions.

Environmental Impact

Pollutants like sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), and ozone (O3) contribute to acid rain and smog formation. This can harm vegetation, soil quality, and aquatic ecosystems.

Economic Costs

Air pollution leads to increased healthcare costs due to higher incidences of respiratory illnesses and other health issues. It can also affect tourism and outdoor recreational activities when air quality is poor.

Climate Change

Some air pollutants, like black carbon and methane, contribute to global warming and climate change. This can have far-reaching effects on weather patterns, sea levels, and ecosystem stability.

Social Impacts

Vulnerable populations, such as children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing health conditions, are particularly susceptible to the health effects of air pollution. Reduced productivity and missed workdays due to illness also impact communities and economies.

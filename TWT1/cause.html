Causes of Air Pollution

Industrial Emissions

Emissions from factories, power plants, and industrial processes release pollutants such as particulate matter (PM), sulphur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and volatile organic compounds (VOCs).

Transportation

Vehicles, particularly those using diesel fuel, emit pollutants such as nitrogen dioxide (NO2), carbon monoxide (CO), and PM. Traffic congestion can exacerbate these emissions.

Biomass Burning

Agricultural practices such as slash-and-burn farming, forest fires, and burning of crop residues release large amounts of smoke, PM, and pollutants like carbon monoxide and VOCs.

Urbanization and Construction

Rapid urban development can lead to increased dust and particulate matter due to construction activities and road dust.

Energy Production

Burning of fossil fuels for electricity and heating contributes to emissions of sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter.
